Welcome

As COVID started back on December 31, 2019, no one predicted the great impact it will have on the whole world. Our lives have changed drastically since then, whether it's our education, social life and mental health. We've all changed one way or another. On this website, a group of 3 students, Asma Alshebli, Jonathan Hackett and Pavel Ghazaryan share their personal experiences with COVID and summarize the measures their countries and university went through to prevent the pandemic from spreading.

Personal Experience

Jonathan

Jonathan is a british foundation year student at The University of Manchester. He gives an insight on how COVID impacted him in the UK.

Pavel

Pavel is a foundation year student from Armenia, studying at The University of Manchester. Pavel writes about the effect COVID had on his life and on Armenia in general.

Asma

Asma Alshebli is an Emirati student enrolled in the foundation year program to pursue her studies in computer science. She shares her experience with COVID in the UAE.

COVID in the United Arab Emirates

The United Arab Emirates puts several rules and regulations to ensure the safety of its citizens during the pandemic. Many tests have been done and citizens are encourages to take the COVID vaccinations at one of the many health centers in the country.

University Response

This page goes over the University of Manchester’s response to the pandemic and the steps it is taking to stop the spread. It also offers useful resources for students currently studying at the university, such as links to official university guidance and information on the rules put in place in order to keep the students working on campus as safe as possible. Also given are some examples of the research that the team at Manchester are doing into the nature of Covid and solutions to the issues it causes.
